Analytics: Roller Coaster Data

December 19, 2020

For this project I analyzed data from the best ranked wooden and steel Roller Coasters in the world between 2013 to 2018. Roller Coasters are thrilling amusement park rides designed to make you squeal and scream! They take you up high, drop you to the ground quickly, and sometimes even spin you upside down before running to a stop. Roller Coasters are often split into two main categories based on their construction material: wood or steel.

Throughout the project you will be able to find:

  • Data cleaning and standardization of suppliers names.
  • Analysis of speed, height, length and inversions of Roller Coasters.
  • Does speed affect the length of a Roller Coaster?
  • Roller Coaster seat type analysis.
  • Analysis of the ranks of Roller Coasters over the years.

Datasets and Technologies Used

The analysis was done with three csv files from Kaggle. ‘roller_coasters.csv’ contains information of Roller Coasters operating between 2013 to 2018 including the name, material type, seating type, speed, height, length, number of inversions, manufacturer, the park where its located and its status.

On the other hand, ‘Golden_Ticket_Award_winners_Wood.csv’ and ‘Golden_Ticket_Award_Winners_Steel.csv’ contain information of the name of the Roller Coaster, its location, supplier, the year it was built, the ranking position, the number of points scored and the year for each of the two categories, wood and steel, respectively.

In terms of technologies, python was used for the data analysis with the following tools: pandas for data tyding and manipulation, numpy for numerical analysis and matplotlib and seaborn for plots and graphs.

Data Analysis

Speed, Height, Length and Inversions of Roller Coasters

Roller Coasters main characteristics are their speed, height, length and the number of inversions. In that sense, I created a function plot_histogram that takes as a parameter a DataFrame and plots a histogram for each numeric column of type ‘float 64’. The function returns a dictionary with the mean and median for each characteristic and plots a histogram for each feature as can be seen on Figure 1.

The distribution of Roller Coaster´s speed is unimodal, with a mean and median close together with 70 km/h and 72 km/h respectively and a slightly symmetrical distribution. Roller Coaster’s height are centered around 26 meters while the length is unimodal with a mean of 606 meters and a median of 500 meters. Since this distribution is right skew is better to use the median as a measure of central tendency since there are outliers of Roller Coasters that have a length of more than 2000 meters. Finally, the distribution of the number of inversions is right skew with the majority centered around [0 to 1] inversions.

Does speed affect the length of a Roller Coaster?

It is important to know if the four main characteristics of Roller Coasters are related to one another for instance, does the speed of a Roller Coaster has an inference on its length? In that respect, I created a function coaster_scatter that takes as parameters a DataFrame and two column names; it plots a scatter plot with the values of each column in the x and y axis respectively and returns the correlation coefficient of the two variables.

Figure 2 shows the scatter plot of Roller Coasters speed Vs length with a regression line of 95% confidence interval, a cube root transformation was applied to the length column to reduce its right skewness which was 1.24 and after the transformation it was standardized to -0.047. Values on both x and y that were NaN were replaced for 0. The correlation between the two variables is 0.72 which means that there is a positive relationship between them, where the higher the speed of a Roller Coaster the higher its length. It can be said, that the higher the speed a Roller Coaster achieves along its course the more space it will need to decline its velocity and make a smooth transition to a full stop.

Roller Coaster Seat Type Analysis

There are 14 different types of Roller Coaster seating types and from them the 5 most common are: Sit Down, Spinning, Inverted, Suspended and Alpine. That being the case, I created a function seating_plot that takes as parameter a DataFrame and plots a bar graph for the count, average speed, average length and average height for the seating types. The resulting plots can be seen on Figure 3 above.

Continuing with what was stated previously, the Sit Down seating type is the most common among Roller Coasters representing 85.6% of top 5 group followed by Spinning (5.8%) and Inverted (4.7%). In terms of average speed the roller coasters with the seating type that goes the fastest is Inverted with 72 km/h,followed by Sit Down with 35 Km/h and Suspended with an average speed of 29 km/h. Inverted seating types need to have a higher velocity to be able to traverse the coaster upside down doubling the speed of the other seating types. Moving forward, the roller coasters with the seating types with the highest average length are Alpine with 803 meters followed by Inverted with 631 meters. Alpine coasters have the highest length due to the fact that they are constructed alongside the descend of mountains, this type is popular in the Swiss and Austrian Alps. Finally, in terms of average height the Roller Coasters that have an Inverted seating are the tallest with 33 meters, followed by Sit Down (26 meters) and Alpine (25 meters). As was the case with average speed, Inverted Roller Coasters are the tallest due to the fact that the passengers traverse the course upside down.

Ranks of Roller Coasters Over the Years

Roller Coasters acquire points during the year and are given a ranked position based on their overall score. That being said, I created a function plot_coasters_ranking that takes as parameters a ranking DataFrame, either wood or steel, a list of Roller Coasters names and a list of park names, since a Roller Coaster can be in different parks around the world. As can be seen below, the function plots a line graph of the rank the parks achieved throughout the years.

El Toro, Boulder Dash and Phoenix compete in the wooden category. These three Roller Coaters have achieved at least once the number one position in the category. Boulder Dash was able to be ranked first for 4 straight years in a row (2013-2016), after that its positioned declined being in third position for 2017 and fourth in 2018. Phoenix has had the greatest comeback, after being in rank four in 2014 it achieved rank two in the category in 2016 and 2017 and rank one in 2018. Meanwhile, El Toro has maintained its position in the number two throughout the years claiming rank one in 2018.
